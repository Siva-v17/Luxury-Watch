Speak the Language

A Collector's Glossary

Six terms worth knowing before your next conversation with an advisor.

Tourbillon
A rotating cage housing the escapement, designed to counteract the effects of gravity on accuracy — now prized as a display of mechanical mastery.
Power Reserve
The duration a watch will run after being fully wound, without further winding or wrist motion.
Côtes de Genève
A decorative striped pattern hand-finished onto movement bridges — equal parts ornament and proof of craftsmanship.
Chronometer
A watch movement certified by an official body (such as COSC) to meet strict precision standards across multiple positions and temperatures.
Complication
Any function beyond basic timekeeping — moonphase, perpetual calendar, chronograph — each adding mechanical and assembly complexity.
In-House Movement
A calibre designed, manufactured and finished entirely within a single maison, rather than sourced from a third-party movement supplier.
