Maison Spotlight

Celeste: Mapping the Night Sky to the Wrist

Founded in 1932 by an astronomer turned watchmaker, Celeste built its first sky-chart dial by hand-painting star positions under a magnifying loupe. Today the house remains the only maison certified to produce true astronomical complications.

The Standard We Demand

In-House Movements

Every maison we represent designs and finishes its own calibres — no third-party movements, no shortcuts.

Independent Ateliers

Family-owned and operated, free from conglomerate production quotas that compromise quality at scale.

Verified Provenance

Every piece arrives with full documentation and certification — no exceptions, regardless of price point.

How We Choose

Earning a Place in the Boutique

Fewer than one in twenty maisons that approach us ever make it onto the shelf.

1

Heritage Review

We trace each house's founding story, archives and continuity of ownership before any other criteria are considered.

2

Movement Audit

Our horologists disassemble sample calibres to verify in-house design, finishing standards and long-term serviceability.

3

Atelier Visit

A senior advisor visits the workshop in person — production quotas, working conditions and quality control all factor in.

4

Ongoing Standard

Acceptance is never permanent. Every maison is re-reviewed annually against the same bar that earned them a place.
