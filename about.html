1898 — Geneva

It Began with a Single Movement

Founder Émile Stackly opened a modest workshop on the banks of the Rhône, determined to build the most accurate pocket chronometer in Europe. Within a decade, royalty across the continent wore his name on their wrists.

Today, that same obsession guides every artisan in our atelier — bound by the conviction that time, when measured beautifully, becomes art.

Every case that leaves our Geneva maison carries 126 years of uninterrupted expertise: from hand-bevelled bridges to grand complications assembled under 40x magnification.

Our Journey

Milestones in Time

1898

The Founding

Émile Stackly opens his Geneva atelier on Rue du Rhône. His first chronometer, the Calibre I, sets a new standard for pocket watch accuracy across Europe.

1932

First Tourbillon

Our inaugural in-house tourbillon astonishes the Basel Salon. Watchmakers from across Europe travel to examine what critics call "an impossible mechanism made inevitable."

1969

Chronometer Record

The Calibre XVIII sets the COSC accuracy benchmark that would stand for over a decade — achieving ±0.3 seconds daily deviation under rigorous testing conditions.

2004

Private Collections Launch

The début of bespoke commissions — no catalogue, no compromise. Each piece built to a single patron's brief over 18 to 36 months, every component unique.

2024

The Modern Maison

32 boutiques across 18 countries, one uncompromising standard. Our Geneva atelier employs 84 master watchmakers — each holding a decade of specialist formation.

Our Philosophy

What We Stand For

◈

Precision

Every component hand-finished to micron tolerances. No deviation leaves our atelier — perfection is a baseline, not a claim.

❖

Heritage

126 years of unbroken craftsmanship. Each generation of artisans trains under the last — knowledge never written down, only worn into the hands.

✦

Rarity

Fewer than 500 timepieces made annually. We choose intentional scarcity — because a Stackly on every wrist would mean a Stackly on no wrist worth wearing.

⬡

Stewardship

Lifetime servicing for every owner. A Stackly is never obsolete — our atelier will restore any calibre, however old, back to original specification.

01
Movement Assembly
Each calibre assembled by a single watchmaker — start to finish, signature on the caseback.
02
Hand Finishing
Côtes de Genève, Anglage, perlage — each surface addressed by hand under 40× magnification.
03
Quality Control
16-day chronometric testing across five positions. Only pieces within ±0.5 seconds leave our atelier.
